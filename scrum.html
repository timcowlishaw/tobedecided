What is Scrum?

Scrum is a product development methodology that embodies the Agile principles, as well as incorporating it's own Three pillars of Transparency, Adaption and Inspection. Scrum recognises that customer requirements may change, and therefore aims to ensure that the team is as responsive as possible to these changes in requirements.

Scrum in IRFS

Scrum gives us a way to frequently check how we're getting on with a project. It allows us to spot problems quickly (at retrospective) and evaluate progress regularly (at the review). This should give us good visibility of how a project's getting on, and opportunities to decide whether to continue with particular pieces of work (or not).

Return on Investment

The return on investment (ROI) for each user story is calculated from the value assigned to each user story devivded by the difficulty on a fibonacci scale

Roles

A scrum team consists of a Product Owner, who is responsible for the Product vision, a Scrum Master, who is responsible for ensuring team follows the Scrum process, and the team - who are everyone else.
